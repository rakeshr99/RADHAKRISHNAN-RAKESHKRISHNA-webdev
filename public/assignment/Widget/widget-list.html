Soldier Richard has a magical gift for the Judges | Britain’s Got Talent 2016

The ascendancy of Richard Jones, the soldier-turned-showman who won Britain’s Got Talent on Saturday after a series of magical performances, therefore came as something of a surprise to professional rivals, some of whom say there was a distinct sense of deja vu watching his final act. The 25-year-old has faced criticism at various stages of the competition for imitating fellow magicians, allegedly borrowing card and sleight-of-hand tricks from legendary British performer Paul Daniels and a similar contestant on America’s Got Talent. Magician Richard Jones wins Britain's Got Talent In his performance in Saturday’s final, Jones, who is still a serving soldier with the Household Cavalry, used military nostalgia in a twist on a classic trick, impressing judges and public enough to become the first magician ever to win the television talent show.
